**Scrum** is one of the most popular **Agile frameworks** used for managing and delivering complex software projects. It emphasizes **iterative development, collaboration, and adaptability**, focusing on delivering value quickly through short, time-boxed iterations called **sprints**.

---

### **Scrum Agile Process**

Scrum organizes the project into **roles, events, and artifacts**:

#### **1. Scrum Roles**



   * Team develops, tests, and integrates features iteratively. Daily **Scrum meetings** track progress.
4. **Daily Scrum (Stand-up)**

   * Short (15-min) daily meeting for team members to share progress, plans, and obstacles.
5. **Sprint Review**

   * Team demonstrates completed work to stakeholders and collects feedback.
6. **Sprint Retrospective**

   * Team reflects on processes, identifies improvements for the next sprint.
7. **Increment**

   * Working software delivered at the end of each sprint.

---

#### **3. Scrum Artifacts**

| Artifact            | Description                                                |
| ------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Product Backlog** | List of all desired work items/features.                   |
| **Sprint Backlog**  | Subset of Product Backlog selected for the current sprint. |
| **Increment**       | Working, tested software delivered at the end of a sprint. |

---

### **Scrum Process Diagram**

```id="5skw1p"
Product Backlog → Sprint Planning → Sprint (1–4 weeks) → Daily Scrum → Sprint Review → Sprint Retrospective → Next Sprint
                           ^-------------------------------------------------------------|
```

**Explanation:**

* Scrum is **iterative**, with each sprint producing a **potentially shippable product increment**.
* Feedback and adaptation happen **continuously** through sprint reviews and retrospectives.

---

### **Advantages of Scrum**

1. **Fast Delivery** – Frequent sprints allow delivering working software early.
2. **Flexibility** – Easily adapts to changing requirements.
3. **High Customer Involvement** – Continuous feedback ensures software meets business needs.
4. **Improved Quality** – Regular testing and reviews reduce errors.
5. **Transparency** – Daily stand-ups and sprint reviews provide clear visibility of progress.
6. **Team Collaboration** – Encourages communication, self-organization, and shared ownership.

---

### **Disadvantages of Scrum**

1. **Requires Experienced Team** – Teams must understand Agile principles and be self-motivated.
2. **Scope Creep Risk** – Frequent changes can lead to unclear scope if not managed properly.
3. **Documentation May Be Limited** – Focus on working software can reduce formal documentation.
4. **Not Ideal for Fixed-Price Projects** – Flexible requirements and iterative scope make budgeting harder.
5. **Time Commitment** – Daily meetings and frequent reviews may be overhead for small projects.

---

✅ **In short:** Scrum is ideal for **complex, rapidly changing projects** where **early delivery, collaboration, and adaptability** are key. Its iterative process ensures continuous improvement and high customer satisfaction, though it requires **discipline and experience** to implement effectively.
